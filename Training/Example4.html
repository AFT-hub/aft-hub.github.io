These people may have attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) , one of the most common reasons children are referred for mental health services in the United States. The primary characteristics of such people include a pattern of inattention, such as being disorganized or forgetful about school or work-related tasks, or of hyperactivity and impulsivity.
